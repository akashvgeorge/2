About Kerala tourism:

Tourism is a significant industry in Kerala, with the state known for its backwaters, hill stations, beaches, and wildlife sanctuaries. The backwaters, a network of interconnected canals and lagoons that run parallel to the Arabian Sea coast, are a major draw for tourists who come to Kerala to experience a leisurely ride on a houseboat or canoe, and take in the natural beauty of the region.
Hillstations like Munnar and Wayanad offer scenic views, tea and spice plantations, and adventure activities such as trekking and camping. Beaches like Kovalam and Varkala attract visitors for their golden sands and turquoise waters. Kerala's wildlife sanctuaries, including Periyar and Silent Valley, offer opportunities to spot exotic species such as tigers, elephants, and monkeys. Overall, Kerala's tourism industry offers a rich and diverse experience for visitors to the state.
