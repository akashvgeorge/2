About Pathanamthitta :

Pathanamthitta is a district located in the southern Indian state of Kerala. It is situated in the hilly region of central Kerala, and is known for its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and religious significance. Pathanamthitta is home to the Sabarimala temple, one of the most famous pilgrimage sites in India. The temple is dedicated to Lord Ayyappa, and is visited by millions of devotees every year, especially during the annual Sabarimala pilgrimage season. The temple is situated on a hilltop, and visitors have to trek through dense forests to reach it. Apart from the Sabarimala temple, Pathanamthitta is also home to many other temples, churches, and mosques, which reflect the rich cultural and religious diversity of the region. The Aranmula Parthasarathy Temple, the St. Mary's Orthodox Syrian Church, and the Cherukolpuzha Sree Vishnu Temple are some of the popular religious sites in the district. Pathanamthitta is also known for its natural beauty, and is home to many scenic spots such as the Pamba River, the Konni Elephant Reserve, and the Perunthenaruvi Waterfalls. Visitors can also explore the many tea and spice plantations in the region, and experience the unique flavors and aromas of Kerala. Overall, Pathanamthitta is a beautiful district that offers a unique mix of cultural heritage, religious significance, and natural beauty.
