About Wayanad :

Wayanad is a beautiful district located in the northern part of the southern Indian state of Kerala. It is situated in the Western Ghats, and is known for its stunning natural beauty, lush green forests, rolling hills, and misty valleys. Wayanad is home to many beautiful waterfalls such as the Meenmutty Falls, Soochipara Falls, and Kanthanpara Falls. Visitors can also explore the many caves, trekking trails, and wildlife sanctuaries in the region, such as the Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary and the Tholpetty Wildlife Sanctuary. The district is also known for its spice plantations, and visitors can take a tour of these plantations and learn about the cultivation and processing of spices such as cardamom, pepper, and coffee. The town of Kalpetta is the district headquarters of Wayanad, and is a bustling town with many shops, restaurants, and markets. Wayanad is also known for its cultural heritage, and is home to many ancient temples and archaeological sites such as the Edakkal Caves, which are famous for their rock engravings and cave paintings. The district is also home to many indigenous tribes such as the Paniyas and Kurichiyas, and visitors can learn about their way of life and traditions. Overall, Wayanad is a beautiful district that offers a unique mix of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventure activities, and is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Kerala.
